# Anthropic Usage Policy — Study Guide

Anthropic's Usage Policy (Acceptable Use Policy / AUP, effective 2025-09-15) governs use of Anthropic's products/services. Generated by NotebookLM from the official policy page as part of an `nlwflow` test run — see [[synthesis/anthropic-vs-openai-usage-policy]] for context.

## Key Ideas

**Three tiers of governance:**

1. **Universal Usage Standards** — apply to everyone. Prohibits illegal activity, attacks on critical infrastructure, cybersecurity threats (malware/ransomware/DoS), weapons development (bio/chem/nuclear), harmful content (CSAM, incitement, self-harm promotion), and election/democracy interference.
2. **High-Risk Use Case Requirements** — mandatory in legal, healthcare, insurance, finance, employment/housing, education (testing/admissions/certification), and journalism. Two requirements: **human-in-the-loop** review before any AI-generated advice/decision is finalized, and **disclosure** that content was AI-assisted, given at the start of each session.
3. **Additional guidelines** — consumer chatbots must disclose their AI nature; products for minors (under 18) follow stricter rules; agentic/autonomous use must still follow every AUP prohibition.

**Enforcement:** a dedicated Safeguards Team can throttle, suspend, or terminate access, block/modify outputs, and reports illegal activity (e.g. CSAM) to authorities. Users report issues via `usersafety@anthropic.com`.

## Glossary

- **AUP** — Acceptable Use Policy, i.e. the Usage Policy.
- **CSAM** — Child Sexual Abuse Material; zero-tolerance, including AI-generated.
- **Human-in-the-loop** — mandatory professional review of AI output before it's finalized, in high-risk domains.
- **Model Distillation** — using one model's outputs to train another; prohibited without authorization.
- **Universal Usage Standards** — the baseline rules applying to all users regardless of use case.
